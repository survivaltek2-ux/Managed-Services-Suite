# What Should Be on an MSP / IT Reseller Website

## Executive Summary

Your MSP or IT reseller website is your single most important marketing asset — every cold email, ad, and social post ultimately drives traffic back to it. A high-converting site does three things: builds immediate trust, clearly explains what you offer and who you help, and guides visitors toward a specific action. This guide breaks down every element you need, from the homepage hero to lead magnets, organized by priority.[^1]

***

## 1. Homepage: The First 5 Seconds Matter Most

The "above the fold" section — everything visible before a visitor scrolls — determines whether they stay or leave. It must answer three questions instantly:[^2]
1. **What do you do?**
2. **How does it help me?**
3. **What should I do next?**

### Key Homepage Components

- **Clear headline and sub-headline** — Avoid jargon. Instead of "Delivering IT Solutions," try something like "Managed IT Support for Law Firms in the Hudson Valley." Specificity wins.[^3][^4]
- **Primary Call-to-Action (CTA)** — Use action-specific CTAs like "Book a Free IT Assessment" or "Schedule a 15-Minute Consultation" rather than generic "Contact Us".[^5][^6]
- **Social proof eyebrow** — A short trust bar above the headline (e.g., star ratings, client count, or a notable client logo) immediately signals credibility.[^7]
- **Supporting imagery or video** — A short explainer video or professional photos of your team increases time-on-site and reinforces authenticity.[^4]
- **Stats that prove performance** — Display metrics like response times, client retention rates, or number of businesses served. This differentiates you from competitors who only make vague promises.[^8]

***

## 2. Services Pages

Each service you offer should have its own dedicated page. A single "Services" page that lists everything is not enough for SEO or for convincing a specific type of buyer.[^6]

### What Each Service Page Needs

- **Clear description of the service** — Use bullet points to break down what's included. Focus on *benefits* the client receives, not just technical features.[^9]
- **Target audience statement** — Explicitly state who this service is for (e.g., "Ideal for medical offices with 5–50 workstations").[^9]
- **Step-by-step process** — Show prospects exactly what happens after they engage you: Discovery Call → Assessment → Onboarding → Ongoing Support. This removes uncertainty and builds trust.[^9]
- **Industry-specific compliance callouts** — If you serve regulated industries, call out HIPAA, CMMC, GLBA, or other compliance frameworks directly on the service page.[^10][^3]

### Core Services to Highlight for MSPs

| Service Area | Why It Matters to Buyers |
|---|---|
| Managed IT Support / Help Desk | Day-to-day reliability; removes the "Who do I call?" question |
| Cybersecurity | Top-of-mind concern for SMBs; high fear factor drives decisions |
| Cloud Services / Microsoft 365 | Familiar to buyers; easy to explain value |
| Backup & Disaster Recovery | Easy to quantify ("What if you lost everything tomorrow?") |
| Compliance (HIPAA, CMMC, etc.) | Creates a niche; hard for generalist MSPs to compete |
| Network Infrastructure | Appeals to growing businesses needing scalable setups |

***

## 3. Trust Signals — The Most Underutilized Section

IT and managed services are trust businesses. A prospect is essentially handing you the keys to their entire digital infrastructure. Social proof is the fastest way to overcome that hesitation.[^11]

### Testimonials
Reach out to satisfied clients and collect short, specific quotes that mention *results*. Generic praise ("They're great!") is far less effective than outcome-based quotes ("Since switching to [Company], we've had zero downtime in 18 months"). Place 3–5 testimonials on the homepage and repeat relevant ones on service pages.[^7][^11]

### Case Studies
A brief 1-page case study (problem → solution → result with a quantified outcome) is one of the most powerful conversion tools on an MSP site. At minimum, aim for one case study per major service or industry vertical served. You can gate longer case studies behind an email opt-in to generate leads.[^12][^5]

### Google Reviews & Certifications
Focus heavily on Google Reviews before worrying about other platforms — aim for at least 50 Google reviews before branching out to Clutch or others. Display vendor certifications (Microsoft Partner, CompTIA, Cisco, etc.) prominently in the footer and on an About page.[^12]

### Company Stats
Hard numbers build confidence fast. Examples:[^8]
- "Average response time: under 15 minutes"
- "98% client retention rate"
- "Supporting 200+ businesses across Orange County"

***

## 4. About Us Page

The About Us page is the second most important page on your website. Buyers want to know *who* they're trusting, not just *what* services are offered.[^4]

- **Team photos and bios** — Real faces destroy anonymity and humanize your brand. Include each technician's certifications and areas of expertise.
- **Company story** — How did you start? What problem were you trying to solve? A genuine origin story builds connection.[^4]
- **Mission and values** — What do you stand for? Reliability? Transparency? Local community?
- **Local roots** — If you serve a specific geographic area, lean into it. Mention neighborhoods, counties, or towns you serve. This also reinforces local SEO.[^13]

***

## 5. Industries / Verticals Page

One of the highest-ROI additions to an MSP website is a dedicated page (or set of pages) for each industry vertical you serve. When a dental office owner searches "IT support for dental practices," a page built around that exact use case will outperform a generic IT services page every time.[^14][^10]

Popular MSP niches to build pages around:[^10][^14]
- Healthcare (HIPAA compliance)
- Legal / Law Firms (data confidentiality)
- Financial Services (SEC, GLBA compliance)
- Government Contractors (CMMC)
- Dental / Medical Practices
- Nonprofits
- Manufacturing

Each vertical page should speak in the industry's own language, name the regulations they face, and reference the specific software or systems they use (e.g., "We support Dentrix, Eaglesoft, and other dental management platforms").

***

## 6. Pricing Page

Pricing transparency is a competitive advantage for MSPs because most competitors hide their prices. You don't have to publish exact per-user costs, but you should give prospects a realistic range or starting price so they can self-qualify before contacting you.[^15]

### Best Practices for MSP Pricing Pages[^16][^17][^15]

- Show 2–3 tiers (e.g., Essentials, Business, Enterprise) with a feature comparison table
- Highlight a "Most Popular" plan to reduce decision fatigue
- Be transparent about what's *not* included to avoid pricing surprises
- If you don't want to publish prices, include a short form or chatbot that delivers a custom quote within 24 hours
- Use "starting at" pricing to set expectations without locking in rates

***

## 7. Lead Magnets — Turning Visitors Into Contacts

Most visitors won't contact you on their first visit. A lead magnet captures their email so you can nurture them over time. The best MSP lead magnets are resources that directly address a pain point your ideal client already has.[^18][^19]

### High-Converting MSP Lead Magnets[^20][^21][^18]

| Lead Magnet Type | Example | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Free Security Assessment | "Get a Free Cybersecurity Risk Audit" | Foot-in-the-door; reveals real vulnerabilities |
| Compliance Checklist | "HIPAA Compliance Checklist for Medical Offices" | High perceived value for regulated industries |
| Cost of Downtime Calculator | "Calculate What an IT Outage Costs Your Business" | Creates urgency with personalized data |
| Buyer's Guide | "10 Questions to Ask Before Hiring an MSP" | Positions you as the educator and trusted advisor |
| Industry-Specific eBook | "Cybersecurity Guide for Law Firms" | Niche targeting; pre-qualifies the lead |
| Webinar / Training | "Free Live Workshop: Protecting Your Business from Ransomware" | Builds relationship before the sales call |

Place lead magnet CTAs on blog posts, service pages, and as exit-intent popups to maximize capture rates.[^22]

***

## 8. Blog / Resource Center

A regularly updated blog serves two purposes: it generates organic search traffic, and it positions your company as a knowledgeable authority.[^3][^22]

### Content Strategy Tips

- Publish 800–1,200 word posts targeting specific keyword phrases your ideal clients search for (e.g., "IT support for small businesses in Middletown NY")[^3]
- Cover timely topics: ransomware trends, new compliance requirements, Microsoft 365 updates
- Repurpose blog posts into email newsletters and LinkedIn posts to maximize reach
- Use a consistent content calendar — one post per week is more valuable than sporadic bursts[^3]

***

## 9. Conversion Tools

### Live Chat / AI Chatbot
Websites with live chat tools convert significantly more visitors into leads. Even a simple AI chatbot that collects visitor info after hours can dramatically increase lead volume. For MSPs, different buyer types prefer different communication channels — give prospects the option to chat, call, or submit a form.[^23][^24][^6]

### Inline Calendar Booking
Replace basic contact forms with a direct calendar scheduling tool (e.g., Calendly or HubSpot Meetings). Offering a "Book a 15-Minute Discovery Call" button reduces friction and allows prospects to commit at peak interest.[^22][^5]

### Multiple Contact Paths
Technical decision-makers often prefer a ticket-style form; executives prefer a direct phone number; and some prospects respond better to immediate chat. Provide all three options with equal visibility throughout the site.[^24]

***

## 10. Technical Foundations

Even the best content won't convert if the site performs poorly.[^25]

| Technical Element | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Mobile responsiveness | 50%+ of web traffic is mobile; Google penalizes non-mobile sites[^25] |
| Page load speed | Slow sites increase bounce rates; compress images and use fast hosting[^13] |
| SSL certificate (HTTPS) | Required for trust; browsers flag HTTP sites as "Not Secure" |
| Local SEO optimization | Claim and optimize your Google Business Profile; build local backlinks[^3][^24] |
| Schema markup | Helps Google display your business info (hours, reviews) in search results |
| Clear navigation | Keep the menu to 5–7 items max; visitors should reach any page in 2 clicks[^2][^26] |

***

## 11. Footer — Often Overlooked

A well-designed footer acts as a trust anchor and navigation safety net for visitors who scroll to the bottom:

- **Phone number and email** — Make contact information impossible to miss
- **Physical address** — Signals legitimacy and helps local SEO
- **Service area statement** — "Proudly serving Orange County, Rockland County, and the Hudson Valley"
- **Vendor/partner logos** — Microsoft, CompTIA, Cisco, etc.
- **Links to key pages** — Services, About, Blog, Privacy Policy
- **Social media icons** — LinkedIn is most relevant for B2B MSPs
